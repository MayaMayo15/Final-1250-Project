Initial Idea + Research

Research and Proposal

The object I will redesign is a desk lamp transformed into a multifunctional clothing rack lamp, preserving its light source while adding physical and emotional functionalities. The primary audience is young adults interested in home decor. Standing at 167 cm, the body uses steel for strength. The flexible neck uses a spiral metal spring wrapped with metal tape. Polypropylene covers the light. This minimalistic design serves as both a clothing rack and mood lamp.

Process Work

Object Attribution Grid

Form - The object takes the form of a floor lamp scaled to the size of a vertical clothing rack approximately 157 cm tall to match the standard closet rod height.

Material - Composed of a sturdy steel material for the body and stand to add weight, and polypropylene plastic for the light to ensure durability and soft lighting.

Appeal - The object appeals to the audience through its clean, minimalistic aesthetic and multifunctional design.

Purpose - Its purpose is to serve as a functional mood lamp and a clothing rack that can hold up to 2-3 heavy garments.

Message - The object conveys a message of style and design, focusing on both aesthetics and companionship rather than extensive mechanical functionality.

Audience - The object is meant for young adults and middle-aged users interested in home decor and ambiance lighting, it is gender neutral and inclusive.

Location - The object can be flexibly placed indoors, ideally near the front door, living room, reading room, or bedroom.

Usability - Its flexible neck allows easy adjustment of light direction, with features like a clicking switch providing clear feedback to the user.

Techniques - The design incorporates Don Norman's interactive principles, such as visibility, feedback, constraints, and affordance, to enhance user experience.

Fundamental Fiction Value

The main intention of this product is to function as a multifunctional clothing rack lamp. It incorporates key functional elements including reduced effort, organization, integration, and sensory appeal. This product reduces the user's effort in finding a place to hang clothes and organizes the environment by serving as a designated clothing rack. Its smart technology element also minimizes disruption by waking users with gentle light instead of an abrupt alarm. Additionally, it integrates multiple product functionalities into one device, making it highly cost-effective and convenient. With its aesthetically pleasing and stylish design, it also delivers strong visual appeal.

Supplement Fiction Value

Its aesthetically pleasing and stylish minimalistic design satisfies the emotional values of design aesthetics and badge value. Owning a stylish product is not only visually pleasing but also elevates self-confidence by serving as a symbol of good taste. Beyond visual appeal, the smart functionalities of this product—such as light alarms and emotional interactions through its adjustable neck—enable emotional values including therapeutic benefits, fun and entertainment, and anxiety reduction. Since the product functions not only as a practical tool but also as an emotional companion in the user's home, it reduces anxiety by alleviating loneliness, adds playful interactivity through engagement with the product's "life," and improves mental wellbeing.

Admirable Fiction Value

What truly sets this product apart from other lamps or clothing hangers are its values of affiliation and motivation. In a not-so-distant imaginative future, our home furniture may all be smart, multifunctional, and aesthetically pleasing. Owning this product would then create a sense of belonging to a community of people who recognize or own this product—similar to the connection among owners of pets of the same species. At the same time, the way this product is personified and brought to "life" can also motivate users in different ways. Seeing the mood lamp display a sad expression could potentially motivate the user to take action and improve their current situation, or it can simply serve as an emotional supporter that accompanies the user through their day.

Prototype 1

Prototype 1 is a very basic and simple prototype of the lamp’s overall form. It has a round base, a flexible neck, and a round light. This prototype was able to represent a very sketchy version of the product's design, however I couldn’t reflect its actual form as the paper neck was too soft and wobbly.

To create this prototype, I started by drawing and cutting two large circles and two smaller circles on a large piece of cardstock paper. I then measured the circumference of the two circles and cut out a paper stripe that was 2 inches wide with a length matching the circumference. After cutting fringes on both sides of the stripe, I applied glue to attach them. I repeated the same process for the lamp using the smaller circles. For the neck section, I cut out two long stripes of the cardstock and folded them on top of each other to create a spring-like material that would allow flexibility.

Prototype 2

The key development in Prototype 2 focused on improving the neck structure so the lamp could stand independently and better showcase the product's form. I also revised the lamp head design from flat to rounded, which created a more aesthetically pleasing appearance and allowed for a larger light projection.

To create a structured neck that could be twisted into different shapes, I repeated the origami folding process, then separated the two stripes and used an awl to pierce a hole in the center of each folded square. Once all the holes were made, I threaded an aluminum wire through them and connected it to the lamp's base and head for stability.

For the rounded lamp surface, I followed a similar process to Prototype 1, but this time created a ring instead of a solid circle. I cut another circle of the same size and made vertical, horizontal, and diagonal cuts halfway to the center. After cutting, I bent the circle to fit securely inside the ring.

Final Prototype

The third prototype refined the lamp section while maintaining the rounded design from Prototype 2. To better showcase the lamp's soft, blurred lighting effect, I switched from cardstock to baking paper, which allowed light to pass through. Testing with my phone's torch demonstrated a significant improvement—the translucent material revealed how the lamp would appear in actual use.

For the final prototype, I created a simple app mockup with two screens showcasing the product's key digital features: alarm settings and remote lamp control. After Bluetooth pairing, users can set automatic lighting times and adjust brightness through a slider interface instead of traditional ringtones.

The second screen allows manual neck adjustment through the app. This interactive experience allows users to toggle, set, and name different neck forms. Users can save multiple designs and simply tap preset moods like "curious" or "happy" to instantly reshape their lamp.

Concept Statement

I am designing a smart multifunctional clothing rack lamp that serves as both a functional storage solution, holding up to three coats or jackets, and an emotional companion. This fictional object provides mood lighting through customizable colors via an app, automatic morning alarms with bright light, and a flexible neck that moves to express emotions like happiness, sadness, or curiosity, mimicking a pet or friend.

I am designing for individuals who value home aesthetics and seek low-maintenance companionship. The product is intended for private indoor spaces, specifically bedrooms or living rooms, where users interact with it everyday individually to foster personal connection and feel special.

The flexible neck embodies Don Norman's visibility principle, making lighting adjustments intuitive and discoverable. A clicking switch provides clear feedback, while neck length constraints ensure physical stability. The familiar switch design and metal-wrapped neck offer strong affordances, initiating naturally intuitive interactions from first encounter.

This object reflects society's growing desire for technology that offers emotional support alongside functionality, suggesting a future where everyday objects become companions that respond to our moods and enhance well-being through thoughtful, human-centered design.
